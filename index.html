About

Research-driven embedded wireless systems

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Engineering at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. My research explores near-field communication, wireless communication networks, embedded systems, RF fingerprinting, and machine learning for wireless security. I am especially interested in practical systems that improve reliability, efficiency, and security in next-generation IoT deployments.

Research Interests

Core areas

Near-Field Communication

Reliable NFC identification, dense tag deployments, ISO15693 anti-collision, and meter-range passive NFC systems.

RF Fingerprinting

Signal-based identification and authentication using physical-layer fingerprints from wireless and embedded devices.

Machine Learning for Wireless Security

Uncertainty-aware learning, domain adaptation, and robust embeddings for practical wireless authentication systems.
